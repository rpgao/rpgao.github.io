    I'm an Assistant Professor in the School of Software Engineering at Beijing Jiaotong University, China. I receive my PhD degree in computer science from Peking University in 2016. My current research interests include mobile intelligent computing, sensor networking, location-based services, and IoT.

Projects

1. Indoor Floor Plan Reconstruction via Mobile Crowdsensing

--Designed computer vision, acoustic sensing, sensor fusion, and probabilistic modeling techniques for fast, resilient, and scalable indoor map construction using smartphones.
--Published in ACM MobiCom'14, IEEE INFOCOM'17, and ACM MobiSys'17.
--Probabilistic models (e.g., particle filter, SLAM), computer vision and mobile sensing algorithms are used for robust user tracking and map generation.

2. Infrastructure-free Smart Device Motion Tracking

--Designed a real-time tracking system which tracks the movement of smartphones in a room with sub-cm accuracy.
--Published in ACM SenSys'17.

3. Indoor Localization via Photo-taking of the environment

--Instead of WiFi signals, we leverage abundant physical objects as reference to triangulate the user location.
--Published in IEEE INFOCOM'14 and IEEE ICC'14.

4. Indoor Vehicle Tracking via Smartphones

--We track the real time movements of vehicles in uninstrumented environments (e.g., underground parking structures) where no radio signal (e.g., WiFi, GPS, cellular) is available.
--Published in ACM Sensys'15 and IEEE GlobeCom'16.

5.Smartphone-based Home Sleep Monitoring

--Design a phone-based E-health system for OSAS, along with energy-efficient SPO2, ECG, and EEG sensors.
--Collaborated with the Sleep Center in Peking University People's Hospital.
